Our Programs

Two rooms, rooted in the Living Garden Approach.

From first steps to kindergarten-ready, our Sprouts and Blooms rooms meet each child exactly where they are — with primary caregiving, low ratios, and a real garden to grow in.

Sprouts Room · 18–36 months

Sprouts Room

Gentle, unhurried days where caregiving is the curriculum. A consistent primary Garden Keeper helps each toddler feel safe enough to explore and brave enough to try.

  • Primary caregiving — one consistent, familiar educator
  • Watering, harvesting & digging in our garden
  • “Heart songs,” sensory bins, water & messy art
  • Photos, naps & meals shared via Brightwheel

Our Philosophy

Different flowers make a beautiful garden.

Our Learning Garden is a space where everyone belongs. Just as different plants and creatures make a garden thrive, different ideas, cultures, languages and learning styles make our community stronger. We call this our Living Garden Approach — learning that is responsive, evolving, and deeply connected to each unique child.

Relationships come first

Every child is supported by a consistent, primary Garden Keeper — a secure base of trust and belonging that makes all other learning possible.

Caregiving is the curriculum

Meals, rest and everyday routines aren't separate from learning — they're exactly where connection, communication and confidence quietly grow.

Rooted in place

On the unceded territory of the W̱SÁNEĆ peoples, we learn outdoors every day — tending real garden beds and raising children who know that to grow is also to care.
