00Brand Essence
"Different flowers make a beautiful garden."

Our Learning Garden is a place where every child — and every family — belongs. Our identity is warm but confident, natural but never washed-out. We trade the expected pastels for richer, braver colour, the way a real garden does.

01Logo & Wordmark

The Sprout Seal, refined.

Our mark is a young sprout that blooms into a marigold — the flower that grows all over our garden. We paired it with a friendlier, modern lowercase sans-serif wordmark (Lato Black), trading the old serif for something warmer, rounder and unmistakably contemporary.

our learninggarden

What changed

Then: Playfair serif, all-caps, formal.
Now: Lato Black, lowercase, modern & approachable — and the seed now opens into a marigold.

The mark alone

The seal works on its own as an avatar or favicon; the marigold is our free-floating accent.

Lockups

Use the horizontal lockup for headers and signage. Stack the wordmark under the seal for square formats and social avatars.

04Motif & Pattern

The marigold, everywhere.

Marigolds grow all over our garden, so they grow all over our brand. The bloom from our seal becomes a flexible motif — scattered, gridded or confettied — for backgrounds, endpapers, packaging, tote bags and wall vinyl.

The hero motif

Built from three rings of petals in marigold, deep-marigold and soft-marigold, with an amber centre. Keep it whole — don't crop into the bloom or change its petal colours. It may sit on cream, sage, raspberry or white.

Garden Scatter · on sage
Marigold Grid · on cream
Confetti · on raspberry

Tip: scale patterns generously — large, breathing blooms feel premium; tiny dense repeats can read busy. For stationery, fade the pattern to 8–14% opacity behind text.

05Iconography

Soft, rounded, hand-on-heart.

Our icons are friendly and solid — rounded corners, no thin hairlines, gentle two-tone fills from the palette. They feel drawn, not engineered. Use them at 1–2 colours on cream or sage, never outlined-only.

07Spaces & Decor

Rooms that feel like a warm hug.

Our spaces translate the brand into the real world: grounded sage, warm cream and natural materials as the calm base, with marigold and raspberry as joyful pops. Honest textures — oak, rattan, linen, wool, terracotta — keep it nurturing, never clinical. Drop inspiration photos onto the boards below to make them yours.

Sprouts Room · 18mo–3

Soft & grounding

A low, calm den for little bodies. Cream walls, a deep-sage reading nook, floor cushions and rattan baskets. A marigold felt garland strings across the window; everything is at toddler eye-level and touchable.

Oak & ply Linen Rattan Sage felt Marigold wool
Blooms Room · 3–5

Bright & curious

A busier, brighter studio for big imaginations. A sage feature wall behind an open atelier of plywood shelving; clear jars of loose parts, real plants, and pops of raspberry on stools and pegs. A marigold motif decal wraps the cubby run.

Birch ply Sage paint Raspberry accent Live plants Marigold decal
Entry & Garden

A marigold welcome

The first thing families see: a hand-painted marigold mural over warm wood cubbies, each tagged with a child's name. Outside, terracotta planters of real marigolds line the path to the garden beds the children tend themselves.

Warm wood Terracotta Marigold mural Sage signage
Decor principles

Calm base, joyful pops · Natural materials over plastic · Everything at child height · Real plants in every room · One marigold moment per space · Display children's real work, not store-bought posters.
